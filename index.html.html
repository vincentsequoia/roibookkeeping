The Promise

We guarantee that your net profit will exceed the industry average for your specific trade — supported by IRS Statistics of Income benchmarks — and we put our money behind that promise. If the guaranteed improvement isn't achieved, we refund the difference in cash.

This is a category-defining commitment. No bookkeeping firm — and virtually no consulting firm — guarantees that clients will achieve above-average profitability for their specific trade. Year 1 — Minimum 1% Profit Improvement

Your baseline revenue is your previous 12 months of total revenue, documented at the start of service. We guarantee your net profit will improve by at least 1% of that baseline. If it doesn't, we refund the difference in cash — up to 1% of baseline revenue.

The guarantee begins after any cleanup or catchup bookkeeping is completed and after 12 full months of ongoing service.

In Years 2 and 3, we guarantee 0.5% of the prior 12 months' revenue in net profit improvement. The guarantee amount decreases because the biggest issues are corrected in Year 1 — but the work of optimizing, refining, and finding new opportunities continues.

From Year 4 onward, the guarantee is tied to the IRS Statistics of Income (SOI) benchmark for your specific NAICS trade code. We continue guaranteeing improvement until your net profit margin exceeds that benchmark by at least 1 full percentage point.

If you reach the benchmark before Year 4, the guarantee ends at that point. The goal is always to get you there as fast as possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my business has a bad year?

If your business experiences a normal downturn, we still honor the guarantee — even if the cause is outside your control. We do not attempt to determine whether a shortfall was caused by market conditions, pricing decisions, or external factors. If the guaranteed improvement isn't met, you still receive your cash refund. Only extraordinary events (natural disasters, major lawsuits, forced relocation, etc.) may pause the guarantee — and even then, the guarantee period is extended, not cancelled.

How do you measure profit improvement?

We use a simple three-step calculation that removes owner compensation decisions from the result — because how much you pay yourself directly affects what shows up as profit on your P&L, and we don't want salary changes to unfairly trigger or void a guarantee refund in either direction.

Step 1: We take your net income from the P&L. Step 2: We add back your actual salary for the year, then subtract your Owner Compensation Baseline — the salary you paid yourself in the prior 12 months, documented and locked at the start of the guarantee year. Step 3: We compare that adjusted figure against the same adjusted figure from the prior year.

If you don't change your salary during the year, this gives the same result as a simple year-over-year comparison. The adjustment only matters when your pay changes — which is exactly when you need the protection. It works both ways: a raise doesn't make your results look worse than they are, and paying yourself less doesn't make them look better. The measurement reflects what your business actually did.

What if I improve profit through my own decisions, not your advice?

We don't debate the source of improvement. This is fair because we carry the risk in both directions — we honor the guarantee even when losses are caused by events outside your control. If the numbers show the guaranteed increase, the guarantee is fulfilled. If not, we pay.

Does the 10% performance fee ever end?

The performance fee continues for as long as you remain a client and your profit exceeds your baseline. The guarantee may sunset when you reach above-average profitability — but your profit growth doesn't have to. The performance fee ensures we stay fully motivated to keep helping you improve, long after the guarantee period ends.

Will I still want your services after the guarantee ends?

By the time the guarantee sunsets, you'll have achieved above-average profitability for your trade. At that point, our value shifts from improving profit to protecting it — which is equally important. You'll already be benefiting from clean books, job costing clarity, cash flow stability, and monthly advisory. Most clients continue to see their margins rise well beyond the guarantee period.

What happens during a recession?

If the Architecture Billings Index (ABI) falls below 45 for three consecutive months — an objective indicator of a collapsing construction pipeline — the guarantee may be paused. It resumes after the NBER officially declares the recession over, plus a 3-month recovery buffer. This protects both sides during severe, industry-wide downturns. Normal slow seasons and business fluctuations do not trigger this clause.

My name is Vincent Rodriguez. Thank you for considering ROI Bookkeeping.

My father and I ran an electrical contracting business together. We were good electricians. Our customers liked us. We worked hard and stayed busy.

My father was the estimator. He had a rule: if he bid the same contractor three times in a row and didn't get the job, he knew his price was too high. If he got every bid, he knew his price was too low. He was brilliant at winning work. But his pricing rule had nothing to do with whether we were making money — it only had to do with whether we were getting the job. That distinction — between pricing to win and pricing to profit — is something I watched play out as his partner. It's one of the reasons I built ROI Bookkeeping.

But looking back, I can see that we were carrying more than we needed to carry.

We didn't track costs by job. We didn't know whether contract work, T&M, or service calls were actually making us money. Cash flow was tight more often than it should have been. Making payroll could be stressful. And because we didn't have the numbers to explain why things felt hard, we just assumed we needed to work harder.

So we did. We worked harder.

Our first bookkeeper was my mother. Then our secretary. They kept enough records to file taxes. That was it. And having family involved in the finances — without the right systems around it — created its own kind of tension. The kind that doesn't stay at the office.

"We weren't failing. We were flying blind. There's a difference."

We had no clear visibility into what was driving profit, what was draining it, or where to focus. Without that clarity, every decision felt like a guess. And running a business on guesses is exhausting — not just financially, but personally.

A Different Vantage Point

I eventually left contracting and spent years as a senior building inspector in California. That role gave me a different vantage point — I watched hundreds of electrical, plumbing, and HVAC contractors operate up close. And I kept seeing the same thing: good tradespeople, working hard, carrying more stress than they should have been. Not because they weren't capable. Because they didn't have the financial systems to match the quality of their craft.

That stayed with me.

When I transitioned into bookkeeping, I made a decision: I was going to build the firm I wished had existed when my father and I were running our business. Not just a firm that keeps accurate books — but one that genuinely helps contractors understand their numbers, improve their margins, and feel more in control of what they've built.

Profit and Peace Are Connected

Here's what I've come to believe: profit and peace aren't separate goals. They're connected.

When you know your numbers, you make better decisions — and better decisions reduce the constant low-grade anxiety of running a business blind. When your cash flow is predictable, payroll isn't a monthly source of dread. When you can see which jobs are actually making you money, you stop second-guessing every bid. When your books are in order, tax season doesn't feel like an ambush.

That's what financial clarity actually gives you. Not just a better P&L. A clearer head. More room to breathe. The ability to work on your business instead of being consumed by it.

"What good is increased profit if it also brings you increased turmoil and less satisfaction in life?"

That question is at the heart of everything we do at ROI Bookkeeping. The guarantee exists because I want you to have proof that this works. The profit focus exists because margin is what funds the life you're trying to build. But the deeper goal — the one that gets me up in the morning — is to help contractors run businesses that don't cost them their peace.

When you work in only three trades, you understand the cost structures, the seasonal patterns, the common margin drains, and the benchmark data well enough to put your money behind a promise.

Terms & Conditions

The Higher-Than-Average Profit Guarantee — These terms govern the Profit Guarantee and Performance Fee structure for all ROI Bookkeeping clients.

Section 1 — Definitions

1.1 Baseline Revenue

The total revenue from the previous 12 months, documented at the start of each service year and recalculated annually.

1.2 Net Profit

Total revenue minus all business expenses including cost of goods sold, payroll, overhead, and operating expenses. For purposes of the guarantee calculation, net profit is adjusted using the Owner Compensation Baseline as defined in Section 1.2a.

1.2a Owner Compensation Baseline

The total W-2 salary and officer compensation paid to the owner during the 12 months immediately preceding the current guarantee year, as documented in the Profit Opportunity Assessment or the prior year's financial records. This figure is fixed for the entire guarantee year regardless of any compensation adjustments made during that year. For new clients, the Baseline is established and signed by both parties during the Profit Opportunity Assessment before the guarantee year begins.

1.3 Profit Improvement

The change in net profit from the beginning of the service year to the end, measured against baseline revenue.

1.4 Guaranteed Amount

  • Year 1: 1% of baseline revenue
  • Years 2–3: 0.5% of baseline revenue
  • Years 4+: The remaining gap until net profit exceeds the IRS SOI benchmark by 1 full percentage point

1.5 IRS SOI Benchmark

The net profit margin for the client's NAICS trade code as published by the IRS Statistics of Income division, based on actual business tax returns. The applicable value is stated in the client's annual engagement renewal.

1.6 Baseline Profit

The client's net profit for the most recently completed tax year prior to onboarding, calculated using the IRS Schedule C or business tax return for that year.

Section 2 — Calculation Method

Profit improvement is calculated using financial statements prepared by ROI Bookkeeping, based on data provided by the client. To ensure the measurement reflects actual business performance rather than owner compensation decisions, the following three-step calculation is used:

Step 1: Take net income from the accrual-basis Profit and Loss statement for the guarantee year. The owner's actual W-2 salary has already been deducted as an expense.

Step 2: Add back the owner's actual W-2 salary paid during the guarantee year, then subtract the Owner Compensation Baseline (Section 1.2a). This replaces actual pay with the fixed baseline, removing compensation decisions from the measurement entirely.

Step 3: Compare the adjusted net income from the current year against the adjusted net income from the prior year, calculated the same way. If the improvement meets or exceeds the guaranteed percentage of baseline revenue, the guarantee is fulfilled.

If the owner does not change their salary during the year, this calculation gives the same result as a simple year-over-year comparison. The adjustment only matters when pay changes — which is exactly when you need the protection. This methodology is fair in both directions: a salary increase does not make results look worse than they are; a salary decrease does not make results look better than they are.
Refund = Guaranteed Amount – Actual Improvement (capped at the full guaranteed amount for that year)

Section 3 — Refund Timing

Refunds are issued within 30 days after the year-end financial review, provided all required financial information has been submitted. Refunds are paid by check or ACH transfer.

Section 4 — Industry Benchmark

The benchmark used for Years 4+ is the IRS Statistics of Income (SOI) net profit margin for the client's NAICS code: 238210 (Electrical) or 238220 (Plumbing & HVAC). The benchmark value is disclosed in the client's annual engagement renewal.

Section 5 — Exclusions

The following do not count toward profit improvement or performance fee calculations. Only operational profit counts.

  • Sale of assets (vehicles, equipment, property)
  • Insurance payouts
  • Legal settlements
  • Government grants or relief programs
  • One-time windfalls not related to normal business operations
  • Owner contributions or capital injections
  • Debt restructuring or refinancing
  • Accounting changes unrelated to operations

Section 6 — Client Responsibilities

The guarantee requires the client to:

  • Provide complete and accurate financial information in a timely manner
  • Respond to requests for documents or clarification within a reasonable timeframe
  • Follow agreed-upon bookkeeping processes and systems
  • Avoid commingling personal and business funds
  • Notify ROI Bookkeeping of major business changes affecting financial reporting
  • Make a good-faith effort to implement recommendations reasonably necessary to improve profitability
  • Participate in quarterly financial review meetings

6.2 Quarterly Good-Faith Review

At each quarterly review, ROI Bookkeeping will assess whether the client is making a good-faith effort to implement agreed-upon recommendations. Both parties will acknowledge this assessment in writing. If cooperation is found to be insufficient, ROI Bookkeeping will issue a written notice with a 30-day cure period. If cooperation does not improve, ROI Bookkeeping may void the guarantee for the current service year and/or issue a 30-day cancellation notice.

This provision is not punitive — it exists to ensure both parties remain committed to the shared goal of improving profitability.

6.3 Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to meet the responsibilities in Section 6 may void the guarantee for the current service year. Either party may discontinue the engagement with 30 days' written notice for any reason, subject to Section 9.

Section 7 — Data Accuracy

The guarantee depends on accurate and complete financial data. If data provided is incomplete, inaccurate, or withheld to a degree that materially affects financial reporting, the guarantee may be voided for the affected period.

Section 8 — Extraordinary Events

If an extraordinary event significantly affects business performance outside normal operations, ROI Bookkeeping may, by mutual agreement, adjust the baseline, adjust the benchmark, pause the guarantee, or agree on a revised measurement method. During a pause, the guarantee period is extended and resumes once normal operations are restored.

Normal business downturns — including slow seasons, lost bids, or general economic softness — do not constitute extraordinary events.

8.1 Systemic Economic Downturn Clause

The guarantee may be paused if: (a) the Architecture Billings Index (ABI) falls below 45 for three consecutive months, or (b) the NBER formally declares a U.S. recession. The ABI is published monthly by the American Institute of Architects; a reading below 45 indicates significant and sustained construction pipeline contraction.

8.2 Resumption After Downturn

The guarantee resumes after the NBER declares the recession over, plus a three-month recovery buffer.

Construction markets historically recover more slowly than the general economy. This buffer protects both parties during the fragile early recovery phase.

Section 9 — Cancellation

9.1 Client-Initiated Cancellation

The guarantee applies only to full 12-month service periods. If the client cancels before completing a full year, the guarantee does not apply and no refund is owed under the guarantee.

9.2 ROI Bookkeeping-Initiated Cancellation

If ROI Bookkeeping initiates cancellation other than for cause, and the client has completed at least 6 months of the current service year, a prorated guarantee refund will be calculated for those months. If cancellation is for cause (client non-cooperation, data withholding, or breach of Section 6), no guarantee refund is owed.

9.3 Mutual Termination

Either party may discontinue the engagement with 30 days' written notice for any reason. In the case of mutual termination, the parties will agree in writing on any applicable prorated refund or guarantee adjustment.

Section 10 — Guarantee Is Not Insurance

The Higher-Than-Average Profit Guarantee is not an insurance product. It applies only to profit improvement as defined in these Terms and Conditions. It does not cover catastrophic business losses, owner decisions unrelated to bookkeeping, business model changes, or events excluded under Sections 5 and 8.

Section 11 — Performance Fee Structure

11.1 Performance Fee

In addition to the monthly service fee, the client agrees to a performance-based fee equal to 10% of annual profit improvement above Baseline Profit (as defined in Section 1.6).

Performance Fee = 10% × (Actual Net Profit – Baseline Profit). If net profit does not exceed Baseline Profit, no performance fee is owed for that year.

11.2 Relationship to the Guarantee

The performance fee is separate from the Profit Guarantee. The guarantee may sunset once the client reaches the IRS SOI benchmark threshold. The performance fee continues regardless of whether the guarantee is active.

11.3 Fairness Clause

The performance fee applies only to profit increases above Baseline Profit. The client is never charged a performance fee on their existing or baseline-level profit.

11.4 Continuity

The performance fee continues for as long as the client remains engaged with ROI Bookkeeping and generates profit above the Baseline Profit.

11.5 Non-Optional

The performance fee is a required component of ROI Bookkeeping's service model and may not be waived.
